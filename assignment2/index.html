D.I.Y Functional Handeld Windmills

Time estimate: 20mins

Skill level: Beginner

Art Hour - Handheld Windmill

Welcome to Art hour with the Berry Bunch Family, this week we are going to be making the cutest handheld windmills; that actually work! They're perfect for weekend afternoons or school holidays when you just need something to do, and even once they're made the fun isn't over yet as now you have a functional windmill to play with.

Here at Berry Bunch Family we know the importance of building fine motor skills in children, the folding of the paper and the threading on to the pipecleaner will help to refine those skills. This craft is also perfect because it doesn't make a mess! There's no glitter or sequins or glue to get everywhere, all the scrap paper can be used up to make the washers and the glue dots make sticking a breeze.

We designed art hour to be simple beginner craft ideas that can all be done within an hour with easily accessible materials. To check out our other art hour tutorials, click on the Art Hour tab on the navigation bar.

Onto the written instructions:

Materials

  • 12.5cm x 12.5cm Foam sheets (& any off cuttings in a different colour)
  • Glue dots
  • Paper straws
  • Pipe Cleaners

Tools

  • Hole Puncher
  • Scissors
  • Glue stick lid (or any round lid that can make an impression)

Method

Making the wheel

  1. Layout your 12.5cm x 12.5cm foam sheet
  2. Fold in half diagonally, unfold, then fold along the other diagonal to create four triangle shaped sections.
  3. Using your hole punch, punch a hole In the centre of the square where the two creases overlap
  4. Cut a slit halfway to the centre of the square along each of the diagonal creases, so that the far edge of each of the triangles from each other while still being connected at the centre.
  5. Punch a hole in the bottom left corner of the triangle facing you, and rotate the foam sheet 90 degrees. Repeat on each side until they all have a single hole.
  6. Stick a glue dot next to each of the holes you just punched, towards the inside of the triangles
  7. Fold one dotted and glued corner into the centre so the two holes line up, and press the glue dot down so it sticks to the centre corner of its triangle section. Repeat for all four sides
  8. At this point, double check to ensure you are able to poke a pipe cleaner through the tunnel you have created.

    9. Making the Washers

  9. Layout an off-cutting of your foam sheet in a different colour to your windmill.
  10. Using a glue stick lid, press down like a cookie cutter onto the foam sheet to make a circular impression.
  11. Use this impression as a guide to cut out a perfect circle, and punch a hole in the centre. Repeat until you have two donut shaped foam circles we will use as washers.

    12. Putting it together

  12. Layout out a paper straw, a pipe cleaner, two donut shaped washers, and your foam wheel.
  13. Wrap one end of the pipe cleaner around the straw 4 times about 15 cm from the top, so it is securely attached.
  14. Thread one of the washers along the pipe cleaner so it sits flush against the straw.
  15. Thread on your foam wheel ensuring that the flat side is not facing out.
  16. Thread on the other washer to rest against the centre of the foam wheel
  17. Cut off the excess pipe cleaner, making sure to leave about 15-20 cm sticking out from the washer.
  18. Wrap the sticking out pipe cleaner around itself in a flat spiral to ensure the washer cannot fall off
