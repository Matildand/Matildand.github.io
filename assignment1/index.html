Assignment 1 - Intereactive Experience Review

Based on this interactive experience - Palat2023

1. The bright background colours were the first things I noticed when loading the page. How the text was changing as the colours moved across the page in line with the mouse hovering. and each of the sections focused on movement

    2. In the two minutes i spent with the experience I:
  • Scrolled up and down to see how the images would change
  • Swiped my cursor across the screen to read the different words connected to each colour
  • Moved my mouse around to see the sensitivity of the moving pixels
  • Repeatedly hovered over the triangle to see the rotation and change of the text
  • Repeatedly hovered over the text at the bottom of the page, rereading to see how the letters moved around
  • Clicking on the text and end of the page anticipating something happening

3. I spent the most time on the first section of the page, moving around the cursor to see the different words that would appear in each quarter of the colours. It engaged me the longest as it was the most changeable and had had deeper interactions for me to discover

4. My most common action while interacting with the page was moving around the mouse and hovering over the images/aspects. There wasn't much interaction related to clicking or anything else.

5. I believe the intended goal of the experience is to display the artist's ability in movement and interactive coding. Each segment appeared to be an exhibit of the range of interactions available within the artist's skillset, the links and contact information at the bottom of the page reinforce the idea of the webpage being a portfolio of sorts.

6. I think the webpage communicates this goal through the excessive nature of all of the aspects. The fact that every detail on the page down to the message at the bottom has a movement attached to it, supports the idea that the page was designed to show off as many skills as possible. The scroll down aspect of the page also gives the impression of a gallery, displaying a wide range of the artist's work.

7. My impression of how the experience should be interacted with is that it is likely designed to be viewed once to provide a quick snapshot of the artist's skills and work, perhaps when trying to decide whether to reach out to hire them for work. The experience may be viewed multiple times to maintain familiarity with the work but ultimately I believe there isn't enough detail or interactions to validate spending an extended period of time on the page.

8. The succinct nature of the webpage and limited examples communicate the idea that this experience is intended to be a short snapshot and should be interacted with as such. If the experience was intended to be revisited or have long periods of time spent on it, it would likely be longer or have more interactions embedded in it beyond hovering and moving the mouse around.

9. The experience doesn't seem to reference many other media forms, due to the simplicity of the design. The simple design reminds me of old websites due to the lack of page borders and the fact that everything exists on the one scroll down. The colour palette used in the four colour split on the opening screen reminds be of the CMY colour palette which are the subtractive primary colours.

10. I think that the almost retro feel and CMY reference is communicating that the experience is handmade, that the artist made it entirely themselves. I think the colour palette implies or references the design industry and therefor communicates that the page should be experienced through a professional lens and by other designers in the field.

11. The general feeling I believe the experience is trying to convey is awe and inspiration. I think this is conveyed through the self made and professional setting the previous references create, as the artist appears to be trying to impress viewers with the skills they are showing off.

12. The most frustrating part of the experience to me is the lack of clickable features. I think that when an asset of a website moves or draws attention to itself I want to be able to click it, so it was disappointing when there were no additional changes that happened when I tried to interact with the page beyond moving around the cursor.

13. The most satisfying interaction to me was the shifting quarters of colours right as you open the webpage. It was satisfying how the colours aligned diagonally, and the borders of each colour were continuously straight and rectangular.
